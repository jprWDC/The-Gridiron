How It Works

You control one team for life. When your team has the ball, you call offense: Run, Pass, Option, or Punt/Field Goal. When they're on defense, you call coverage: Man, Zone, or Blitz.

The rock-paper-scissors: Running into a Blitz gives massive yards (5–22) but a brutal 12% fumble rate — so Blitz is a real gamble for the defense. Passing vs Blitz offers big completions (up to 38 yards) but 14% INT risk including pick-sixes. Man defense stuffs both run and pass hardest, but gives up more sacks and fewer big plays. Zone is the safest call on both sides: balanced yardage, low turnover risk.

Voting: All fan votes accumulate during the 15-second window. The most-voted option wins. Your individual vote takes priority if you submitted one before time ran out.

Defensive calls are private — your fanbase chooses coverage, but the opponent's fans don't see what you called. The outcomes (fumble/INT rates) are determined invisibly by the matchup.

Game clock burns 40–63 sim-seconds per play, targeting ~17 real minutes of play. Hard stop at 25 real minutes. Ties stand in regular season — no overtime.
